Childhood

I have mixed feeling about this time of age. When I was at grade 8, I started skipping classes to hang out with classmates. Although my grades and my intentions weren't at all bad, I was at the lowest of my life, but then I changed school which lead to me changing as well. At the new school, I met great people and we regard each others as close friends. We would play games together, hang out, do sports and feast. Academically wise, I was at my prime. I was the kind of student who never studies but knew most of the lessons taught. League of Legends was the game I was addicted to, so I got carried away and focused less on studying until I got an unsatisfactory result in my national exam back in 2019, a C. Apart from that, everything seems amazing and it stores some of the greatest memories ever.

Teenager life

Adult life was not as different as my teenage years. I started university last year at RULE majoring law. Although I was good at science, my parents wanted me to learn law as they both did. I got a place at ITC and at KIT, and at the time, did not know about PIU, so I missed the scholarship exam. Stuyding law was really fun for a time, until it wasn't. I am the kind of student who does not want to read but listens, and in law school, I had to read. It was hard for me to try hard for the first time, so by the 4th week my year 2 law major, I dropped out, took the exam here at Paragon with the approval of my parents. Coming here taught me that I need to work hard to get to a place that is good. Most of my life I never really worked hard or to my best potential and now it is time to change my attitudes. I am not quite there yet, but I am trying to improve.
